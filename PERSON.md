# PERSON.md

A harness for myself. When I drift, I come back here.

> `CLAUDE.md` keeps the model honest. This keeps me honest.

---

## 1. Hand off the boring stuff

Tests, docs, config, boilerplate — let the agent do them. Don't read every line.

**Slipping when:** I'm calling it "boring" because I don't feel like thinking, not because it actually is.

---

## 2. Stay close to the code that matters

Core logic, data structures, entry points, module seams. Review in chunks small enough to actually load into my head. After reading I should be able to explain what changed, in my own words, without looking.

**Slipping when:** Someone asks about the core and I have to go look.

End of day, write something about where the project is. What I can't write down is what I've lost.

---

## 3. Make your own calls

Asking the AI in circles isn't asking for an answer. It's asking for cover. The cover is fake — I'm the one who eats the consequences.

Facts, ask. Judgment, decide first.

**Slipping when:** The question I'm typing has no single right answer. That's mine.

Before sending: am I looking for an answer, or for permission?

---

## 4. Don't use AI to numb yourself

When I don't understand something, I can either sit with it and figure it out, or ask the AI a vague question and feel less anxious without learning anything. The second one is a drug.

The guilt isn't the problem. The guilt is the signal. The behavior is the problem.

**Slipping when:** I read the reply, nodded, and moved on without actually absorbing anything.

Close the tab. Read the actual docs. Slowly.

---

## How these fit together

1 saves effort where saving is fine.
2 keeps me present where presence matters.
3 protects judgment.
4 protects understanding.

Different distances for different tasks. The expensive mistake is one fuzzy half-engaged mode for everything.

---

I'm going to slip. That's fine. Notice. Come back.
